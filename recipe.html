Chao fan Recipe

Chao Fan, meaning "fried rice" in Chinese, originated in China (likely Jiangsu province) around the Sui Dynasty (589-618 AD) as a way to utilize leftovers. It spread globally, becoming a popular Chinese-inspired, stir-fried rice dish characterized by wok-fried, day-old rice, eggs, meat, and vegetables, often featuring soy sauce.

Ingredients

  • Egg
  • Rice
  • Ground pork
  • Soy sauce
  • Carrots
  • Spring onions
  • Liquid Seasoning
  • Cooking oil
  • Peas & Corn
  • Water spinach
  • Onions & Garlic (Minced)

Basic Instructions (Beef/Pork Chao Fan):

  1. Prepare Egg: Heat oil in a wok and scramble 1-2 eggs until cooked; remove and set aside.
  2. Sauté Aromatics & Meat: In the same wok, heat more oil. Sauté garlic and onions until fragrant. Add ground beef or pork and cook until browned.
  3. Add Veggies: Add diced carrots, celery, or peas and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until tender.
  4. Stir-Fry Rice: Add day-old rice to the pan, breaking up any clumps. Stir-fry over high heat until well mixed and heated through.
  5. Season: Pour in soy sauce, oyster sauce, salt, and pepper to taste. Mix well.
  6. Finish: Return the cooked eggs to the pan. Add sesame oil and chopped green onions, stirring briefly before serving.

Additional Info

  • Prep Time: 5-10 minutes (chopping onions, garlic, and preparing sauces).
  • Cook Time: 10-15 minutes (stir-frying in a wok or large pan).
  • Total Time: 15-25 minutes

Typical Servings

  • Serving Size: 3-4 people
  • Rice Requirement: Usually uses 3-5 cups of leftover, cold, day-old rice.

Adobo is a beloved Filipino dish and cooking process—often considered the unofficial national dish—characterized by braising meat, seafood, or vegetables in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns.
