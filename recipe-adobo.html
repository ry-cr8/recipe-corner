Chicken Adobo Recipe

Adobo is a beloved Filipino dish and cooking process—often considered the unofficial national dish—characterized by braising meat, seafood, or vegetables in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. It is known for its savory, tangy, and slightly sweet flavor profile, with thousands of regional variations existing across the Philippines.

Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Soy sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Garlic
  • Bay leaves
  • Peppercorns

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, combine chicken, soy sauce, and minced garlic. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Start Cooking: Transfer the marinated chicken into a pan. Add vinegar, bay leaves, and peppercorns. Do not stir yet.
  3. Simmer: Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer for about 20-30 minutes until the chicken is tender.
  4. Reduce the Sauce: Continue cooking until the sauce thickens slightly. You can stir at this stage.
  5. Optional Frying: For extra flavor, remove the chicken and lightly fry it, then return it to the sauce.
  6. Serve: Serve hot with steamed rice.
